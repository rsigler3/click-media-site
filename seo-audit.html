The 10-Point SEO Checklist:
Is Your Website Invisible to Jamestown Customers?

Use this actionable checklist to discover what's holding your website back from dominating local search results.

1. Google Business Profile Audit

  • ✓ Is your profile claimed and verified?
  • ✓ Do you have at least 10 high-quality photos uploaded?
  • ✓ Are you responding to every review (even the bad ones)?

Why it matters: Your Google Business Profile is often the very first thing potential customers see when they search for your services. An incomplete profile signals to Google that you might not be an active business, dropping you in Map Pack rankings.

2. The "Mobile-First" Test

  • ✓ Does your site load in under 3 seconds on a 4G connection?
  • ✓ Are buttons large enough to tap with a thumb?

Why it matters: Google actively penalizes websites that are slow or difficult to use on smartphones. Over 60% of local searches happen on mobile devices—if your site isn't optimized for them, you're losing more than half your potential customers.

3. The H1 Header Check

  • ✓ Does your main headline include your primary service + your city? (e.g., "Plumber in Jamestown, NY")

Why it matters: The H1 tag is the most important piece of text on your page. If your main headline just says "Welcome to our website," Google has no idea what you do or where you are located. Be explicit.

4. NAP Consistency

  • ✓ Is your Name, Address, and Phone identical on your website, Facebook, and Google Maps? (Even "St." vs "Street" matters!)

Why it matters: Search engines use NAP (Name, Address, Phone) data to verify your business's legitimacy. Inconsistent information confuses the algorithm and hurts your local search rankings.

5. Meta Title & Description

  • ✓ Do your search results actually "sell" the click, or are they just the name of the page?

Why it matters: Even if you rank #1, nobody will click your link if the description doesn't offer a compelling reason. Think of your meta title and description as a mini-billboard on the search results page.

6. Image Optimization

  • ✓ Are your images huge files slowing down the site?
  • ✓ Do they have descriptive "Alt Text" for Google's bots?

Why it matters: Large images are the #1 cause of slow websites. Compressing them speeds up your site, and adding "Alt Text" (hidden descriptions) tells Google exactly what the image is about, helping you rank in Image Search.

7. Content Depth

  • ✓ Do your service pages have at least 500 words of helpful, expert information?

Why it matters: Thin content pages tell Google that you aren't an authority in your field. To rank well, you need comprehensive, well-written content that thoroughly answers the questions your potential customers are asking.

8. Internal Linking

  • ✓ Do your pages link to each other, or are they "islands" that Google can't navigate?

Why it matters: Internal links help Google's crawlers understand the structure of your website and establish a hierarchy of importance. They also keep visitors on your site longer, which is a positive ranking signal.

9. SSL Security

  • ✓ Does your URL start with HTTPS? (Google will flag "Not Secure" sites and scare away customers).

Why it matters: Security is a top priority for Google. If your site doesn't have an SSL certificate, browsers will literally display a warning to users that your site is unsafe, instantly killing trust and conversions.

10. The Call to Action

  • ✓ Is there a clear, bold button on every page telling the user exactly what to do next?

Why it matters: SEO brings people to your door, but your website's job is to invite them inside. Without clear next steps (like "Get a Quote" or "Call Now"), visitors will leave without taking action.
